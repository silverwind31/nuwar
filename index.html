When war approaches (declaration
of a state of emergency in the
country, mobilization of troops, etc.) ,
the entrances to all defense
structures will be marked with
“Shelter” signs and route signs will
lead to them.

What to do if you are far
from the metro and
your shelter at the
time of the alarm?

1
Go down to the nearest basement or underground car park.
If there is nothing nearby, you can lie down in natural recesses of the terrain: ravines, pits, roadside ditches, or simply on the ground, if you are not at all lucky with shelter.
2
Lie face down with your eyes closed and never look at the sky as the explosion flashes will cause permanent damage to your eyes.
3
Cover your nose, mouth, and all exposed parts of your body with clothing, and keep your hands under you.
Dust from the explosion will cause zero visibility.
4
If you survive the shockwave and get back on your feet, go upwind, you need to find cover as soon as possible.
Why against the wind? Where
a nuclear explosion has occurred, the wind will blow because a layer of hot air rises above the epicenter, drawing in cold air from all sides.
5
Approximately 20-30 minutes will be at your disposal to leave the epicenter and take cover before the radioactive smoke from the fungus falls to the ground, which will significantly reduce your chances of survival.
